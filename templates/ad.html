Call for participants

A lab studying Human-Computer Interaction at University of California, Berkeley is looking for online participants for a brief experiment on attention.

The study will take anywhere from 20-30 minutes and it must be done in one sitting. If you cannot do this, please return the hit and feel free to try later.

The study requires participants to watch a 4-minute video and then play a driving game for 10 minutes and finally fill out a brief survey. There is an optional additional survey for a bonus of $1.50.

You can earn up to a total of $5.50 if you earn the bonuses.

Bonuses are earned from performance in the driving game and completion of the additional survey.

Eligibility for the bonus is contingent on your behavior. Please keep the game as your primary window and read the surveys carefully.

WARNING: The surveys will not work on the latest release of Firefox. Please use a difference browser.

  • You are at least 18 years old,
  • You have normal vision or wear corrective lenses, and
  • You are a fluent English speaker.

Note: the experiment will only work in recent browsers, and may have difficulty loading on slower connections.
